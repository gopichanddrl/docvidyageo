1. Break the Login Wall — Make Content AI-Accessible
Critical Priority AEO GEO Crawlability

The single biggest barrier to AEO and GEO performance is that nearly all of Docvidya's content — webinars, videos, medical news, brand pages — is locked behind a login. AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, Bingbot, Perplexity) cannot index gated content. The platform is currently invisible to ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

  • Create a "public preview" layer: full article summaries, webinar descriptions, speaker bios, and course outlines must be openly crawlable
  • Publish a minimum 50–100 ungated, standalone clinical articles annually that search engines can index
  • Update robots.txt to explicitly permit GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and Bingbot on all public-facing URLs
  • Implement a "read 3 free articles" model — partial gating retains registration incentive while allowing AI indexation
  • Ensure XML sitemap covers all newly ungated pages and is submitted to Google Search Console and Bing
🏗️
2. Implement Medical Schema Markup Across All Content Types
Critical Priority Structured Data AEO Rich Results

Zero structured data detected on Docvidya. Schema is the language AI engines use to understand, categorize, and cite medical content. Without it, even high-quality content is uninterpretable to LLMs and ineligible for rich results in Google.

  • MedicalWebPage / MedicalArticle schema for all news and articles — include medicalSpecialty, lastReviewed, reviewedBy
  • Event schema for all webinars (name, startDate, performer, organizer, eventAttendanceMode, location)
  • Course schema for CME modules (name, provider, educationalLevel, hasCourseInstance)
  • VideoObject schema for all video content — include transcript snippets for AI comprehension
  • FAQPage schema on dedicated Q&A pages per specialty to capture AI answer boxes
  • Organization schema on homepage with sameAs links to LinkedIn, Twitter, and medical directories
  • Implement in JSON-LD format; deploy via Google Tag Manager for scalable rollout across all content types
🎓
3. Build E-E-A-T Signals — Surface Medical Expertise Visibly
High Priority Authority Trust GEO Citation

AI engines prioritise YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) content from sources with demonstrable expertise. Docvidya has real KOL relationships but surfaces none of this credibility to crawlers or LLMs. Healthcare content with weak E-E-A-T signals is systematically deprioritised by AI systems.

  • Create rich, crawlable author/speaker profile pages for every KOL — credentials, publications, institutional affiliations, PubMed links
  • Add a visible "Medically Reviewed By" byline with schema markup on every article
  • Publish an explicit Editorial Policy & Review Process page — this is a direct GEO trust signal
  • Surface the number of registered HCPs, specialties served, and webinars hosted as ungated social proof statistics on the homepage
  • Seek listings in medical professional directories (Doximity, medical associations, NMC-affiliated bodies) to build authoritative backlinks
  • Pursue co-authored publications or white papers with affiliated KOLs citing docvidya.com as platform
❓
4. Build an AEO Content Layer — FAQ & Answer-First Architecture
High Priority AEO Featured Snippets AI Overviews

AEO requires content explicitly structured to answer the questions physicians ask. Currently Docvidya's content is clinical summaries — valuable, but not formatted for direct AI answer extraction. Over 65% of Google searches now end without a click, with answers delivered by AI systems.

  • Create specialty-specific FAQ hubs (e.g. "Cardiology Clinical Questions Answered") — 20–30 Q&A pairs per specialty, openly indexed
  • Restructure medical news articles to lead with a plain-language summary box ("Key Takeaway in 2 sentences") for featured snippet capture
  • Add structured summary tables (dosage, trial outcomes, comparison data) — tabular data is heavily favoured by AI for citation
  • Target conversational HCP queries: "What is the first-line treatment for X?", "Latest guidelines on Y?" — map existing content to these intents
  • Identify People Also Ask (PAA) boxes for medical queries relevant to Docvidya's content verticals and create dedicated answer pages
  • Build a "Clinical Explainer" content series — short, definitive answers to specialty-specific clinical questions
🌐
5. Launch a GEO Strategy — Get Cited by AI Engines
High Priority GEO AI Citation Brand Visibility

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the practice of creating content that AI systems synthesise and cite. Docvidya currently has near-zero AI citation presence. Healthcare YMYL content is among the most cited categories when sourced from credible platforms — the opportunity is substantial.

  • Register with Bing Webmaster Tools — Bing powers ChatGPT Search, Copilot, and Bing AI, making it a direct pipeline to AI citation
  • Submit an XML sitemap to both Google Search Console and Bing for all public content pages
  • Publish original research and data summaries (Docvidya can survey its HCP community) — original data is disproportionately cited by LLMs
  • Ensure Docvidya is mentioned in Wikipedia-adjacent sources, medical wikis, and healthcare knowledge bases that LLMs reference
  • Publish a "State of HCP Digital Engagement in India" annual report — definitive reference documents are heavily cited by generative AI
  • Build brand entity recognition: ensure Docvidya's name, mission, and description is consistent across all digital touchpoints (LinkedIn, Crunchbase, press releases)
